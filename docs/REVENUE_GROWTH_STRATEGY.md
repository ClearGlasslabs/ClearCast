## 1. Executive Summary

ClearGlassInc Artemis already owns four assets most security boutiques never finish:

1. A premium static website with legal, trust, and services pages.
2. A deep documentation library (governance, IR, access control, vuln disclosure, data handling, change mgmt, SOC checklists).
3. A platform blueprint (Artemis self-evolving / Palantir-style full-stack).
4. A GitHub-native automation surface (revenue content bot, agentic bot blueprints).

Translation: we are not "starting from zero." We are packaging an existing artifact library into priced offers and pointing qualified buyers at them. The fastest dollars come from productized consulting + documentation packages; the durable dollars come from retainers and content-driven inbound.

## 2. Fastest Monetization Path (0 to first paid invoice in <= 14 days)

Sell a **Security & Governance Quick Audit** as a fixed-fee, 5-business-day engagement.

- Price: $2,500 flat (intro), $4,500 standard, $7,500 with remediation roadmap.
- Why it sells fast: founders and CTOs at seed/Series A buy "audit + roadmap" because it unblocks their next investor conversation, SOC 2 prep, or enterprise sales motion.
- Why we can deliver fast: the deliverable is assembled from existing repo artifacts (`SECURITY_OPERATIONS_CHECKLIST.md`, `INCIDENT_RESPONSE_POLICY.md`, `ACCESS_CONTROL_POLICY.md`, `DATA_HANDLING_STANDARD.md`, `VULNERABILITY_DISCLOSURE_POLICY.md`, `RISK_OWNERSHIP_GOVERNANCE.md`) plus a tailored gap analysis.
- Payment path: Stripe Payment Link or Stripe Invoice -> 50% deposit, 50% on delivery. No new tooling required.

This is the wedge. Everything else (retainers, productized docs, AI workflow audits) attaches to clients who already paid for the audit.

## 4. Target Customers

Primary ICP (Ideal Customer Profile):
- Seed to Series B B2B SaaS, fintech, healthtech, or AI-native companies (15-200 employees).
- Triggered by: enterprise RFP, SOC 2 / ISO 27001 prep, investor due diligence, or first AI feature shipping to production.
- Buyer: Founder / CTO / Head of Security / Head of Engineering.

Secondary ICP:
- Mid-market companies (200-2,000 employees) with no dedicated security architect, evaluating zero-trust or SOC modernization.
- African and global-south enterprises that want a credible regional partner with international-grade documentation.

Tertiary ICP:
- Exec teams and boards that want a monthly written cyber briefing they can actually read.

We do **not** chase: Fortune 500 procurement (long sales cycle, wrong leverage for current stage), regulated defense work (clearance gating), or pure staffing/body-shop work.

Three message tracks:

1. **Investor / RFP-readiness track**

## 6. Funnel: Traffic -> Payment

**Top of funnel (attract)**
- LinkedIn long-form posts (3 per week) repurposed from existing docs.
- GitHub: pin the public artifact pages (zero-trust playbook, SOC scorecard, executive briefing template).
- SEO landing pages built from `SEO_METADATA.md` targeting: "SOC 2 readiness audit," "AI workflow governance," "fractional CISO," "zero-trust rollout."

**Middle of funnel (capture)**
- Free download: "Security Starter Kit (lite)" -> email opt-in.
- Add a simple lead form on `services.html` and a CTA on `index.html` ("Book a 20-minute Security Readiness Call").
- Calendar tool: Cal.com or Google Calendar appointment schedule for the 20-minute call.

**Bottom of funnel (convert)**
- 20-minute call -> diagnostic questions -> scoped Quick Audit proposal within 24h.
- Stripe Payment Link or Stripe Invoice for 50% deposit. Engagement starts on deposit.
- Delivery via shared Google Drive / GitHub private repo + a 60-minute readout.

**Post-sale (expand)**
- Default upsell at audit close: monthly retainer ($1,500 briefing, $4-8k advisor).
- Quarterly check-in email with one new artifact.

KPI through the funnel:
- Visit -> opt-in: 4%
- Opt-in -> call booked: 8%
- Call -> proposal sent: 70%
- Proposal -> closed: 30%

At 1,000 monthly visits that is ~1 paid audit / month. At 4,000 monthly visits and a tightened funnel, 4-6 audits / month plus retainer attach.

## 7. Automation Opportunities (safe, compliant)

In-scope automations:
- **Lead capture -> CRM**: Form on site -> Google Sheet or HubSpot Free -> Slack/email notification.
- **Proposal generation**: Templated proposal in Google Docs or Notion, auto-filled from intake form (`CLIENT_ENGAGEMENT_INTAKE_TEMPLATE.md`).

- **Nurture sequence**: 5-email sequence to the opt-in list (see Section 9).
- **KPI dashboard**: Lightweight Google Sheet pulling Stripe + Cal.com + site analytics weekly.

Out-of-scope (rejected):
- Scraped-list cold email blasts.
- LinkedIn auto-connect / auto-DM bots (TOS violation).
- Fake testimonials, fake scarcity countdowns, fake "AI-generated client logos."
- Anything that impersonates a regulator, auditor, or platform.

## 12. Daily "Next Best Revenue Action" Heuristic

Every morning, pick one and only one of these, in this order of priority:
1. If a proposal is outstanding and unsigned for >48h, follow up.
2. If a discovery call is booked today, prep the one-page scope before the call.
3. If <2 calls are booked for the week, do 5 ethical outbound touches.
4. If outbound is healthy but the funnel is dry, publish one piece of content.
5. If everything is healthy, productize one more deliverable (turn a custom output into a repeatable SKU).

This keeps the work biased toward cash, not toward perpetual "content for content's sake."

## 13. Risk & Compliance Guardrails

- Every claim on the website must be defensible. No "trusted by Fortune 500" without permission.
- Client work governed by a written engagement letter. Use `CLIENT_ENGAGEMENT_INTAKE_TEMPLATE.md` + a short MSA.
- All outbound complies with CAN-SPAM, GDPR, and platform TOS. Opt-out in every marketing email.
- No security testing of third-party systems without written authorization.
- Affiliate / sponsorship disclosures are explicit on any content where money changes hands.
- AI-assisted deliverables are reviewed by a human before sending.
